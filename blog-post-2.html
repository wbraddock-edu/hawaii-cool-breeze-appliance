Extending Appliance Life in Hawaii's Tropical Climate

By Cool Breeze Team · May 26, 2024 · 6 min read

The average appliance on Hawaii Island faces harsher conditions than virtually anywhere else in the continental United States. Volcanic sulfur dioxide (vog), salt air in coastal areas, high humidity, and hard water all conspire to shorten appliance life. But with proactive maintenance, you can significantly extend how long your appliances last and reduce the frequency and cost of repairs.

Refrigerator Maintenance

Clean condenser coils every 6 months. Dirty coils force the compressor to work harder, increasing energy consumption and wear. The coils are typically located at the back of freestanding models or beneath the front grille on built-ins. Use a coil brush or vacuum to remove dust and debris. Check door gaskets regularly for cracks or tears that allow warm air infiltration. Replace gaskets when they lose their sealing ability. Keep the refrigerator temperature at 37-40°F for the refrigerator compartment and 0°F for the freezer.

Washer Maintenance

Clean your washer monthly using a washing machine cleaner or a cycle with hot water and two cups of white vinegar. In Hawaii's humidity, the drum and dispensers can develop mold quickly. Leave the door open after each use to allow complete drying. Check water inlet hoses annually for cracking or bulging and replace every 5 years regardless of condition — a burst hose causes significant water damage. Clean the lint filter if your washer has one.

Dryer Maintenance

Clean the lint trap after every single load — a blocked lint trap is the most common cause of dryer inefficiency and a fire hazard. Clean the dryer vent duct annually. In Hawaii, where dryer vents are shorter runs in smaller homes, lint can accumulate quickly. A blocked vent causes the dryer to overheat, extending drying time and wearing out heating elements and thermostats prematurely. Consider having your vent professionally cleaned if you notice drying times increasing significantly.

Dishwasher Maintenance

Hawaii's hard water deposits mineral scale on spray arms, the heating element, and the interior. Run your dishwasher monthly with a cup of white vinegar on the top rack (no dishes) to dissolve mineral deposits. Use dishwasher cleaner tablets quarterly. Clean the filter at the bottom of the tub monthly — food debris in the filter causes cleaning problems and can damage the pump.

AC Maintenance

Hawaii's humid air causes evaporator coils to accumulate mold and bacteria faster than in dry climates. Clean or replace filters monthly (not the recommended quarterly or annually for dry climates). Check drain lines quarterly — blocked drain lines cause water overflow that damages cabinets and floors and can short electrical components. Have your AC professionally serviced annually for refrigerant level check and coil cleaning.
